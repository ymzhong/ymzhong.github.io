YIMING ZHONG

I am a KICP fellow at Kavli Institute for Cosmological Physics at the University of Chicago. My research area is in the intersection of theoretical particle physics, astrophysics, and cosmology. I received my PhD degree under the supervision of Dr. Rouven Essig at C.N. Yang Institute for Theoretical Physics, Stony Brook University in 2016. Here is a short summary of myself:

Research Interests

  • Primordial non-Gaussianities
  • Non-cold dark matter
  • Galactic dynamics
  • Experimental searches for dark matter and dark sector particles
  • The origin of the first supermassive black holes
  • The origin of the Galactic center gamma-ray excess
